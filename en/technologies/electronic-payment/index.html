    Development and implementation technologies of the System of Electronic Payments (SEP).

    The System of Electronic Payments (SEP) is the national payment system, which provides the performance of payments between the bank institutions, Treasury Department authorities on the territory of the country with the application of the electronic means of acceptance, processing, information transmission and protection.

    For reference: it takes about 40 minutes (not more) to transfer money from one bank to another bank within one region, and it takes not more than 2 hours for interregional transfer (at the average 1 hour and 10-20 minutes).

    The main tasks of the System are the following:

    • satisfaction of the economic needs, being reformed and developed
    • improvement of the credit and the monetary policy pursued by the National Bank, by receiving the real-time and accurate information about the movement of funds and the state of correspondent accounts
    • execution of all kinds of the interbank cashless settlements
    • minimization of time for the interbank settlements and the capital turnover
    • high level of protection of the interbank settlements
    • provision of a wide variety of services to users
    • high level of the internal accounting and control
    • minimization of the cost of the mediation between banks thanks to optimization of the means of payment and the systems rationalization.
